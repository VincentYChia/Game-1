# Framework B: The Weight of Older Skies

**Archetype shell:** Dim post-apocalyptic weird sci-fantasy (Salvage-Age Fallen Civilization).
**Filling:** The ground is the compacted corpse of civilizations that tried to transcend physics; every "element" you craft with is contamination leaking from a different category of their broken engines. Elementals are not gods. They are exhaust.
**Tonal register:** dim, damp, hush-before-revelation, morally unmoored, wonder-adjacent-to-dread.

---

## 1. Premise & Twist

You live on a grave so old it has become geography. The prior world — perhaps several, stacked — reached too far into the architecture of reality and something load-bearing gave way. What remains is not ruins but *strata*: hills are collapsed arcologies, rivers run through cooling-channels, soil is powdered code.

**The twist:** the eight elements are not natural forces. They are categories of leakage from different kinds of Old World engine. When you wield them, you vent a machine that is still, somewhere, trying to finish its original job.

## 2. Cosmology — The Eight Leakages

Physics mostly works. The exceptions are **the Leakages** — categorical intrusions where Old World engines vent their unfinished labor into the living strata. To craft is to tap a leak. To cast is to stand in the exhaust. The real names of the engines are unknown; the guilds use working names and disagree on those.

- **Physical (Kinetic-Residue):** Not a leak — the default substrate, the one honest thing. What you fall back on when the other seven betray you.
- **Fire (Furnace-Breath):** Vented heat from engines that once smelted the foundations of the sky. It consumes *meaning*; the ash is always a little lighter than the thing it came from.
- **Ice (Null-Draft):** Leakage from engines that refrigerated time, or something adjacent. A bitten tongue forgets the word for *tongue*. Winters near a vent are measured in held breaths.
- **Lightning (Throughline):** Discharge from severed signal-engines pulsing to complete a circuit across the dead substrate. A caster is a gap it would like to close.
- **Poison (Gardener-Error):** Biological programs gone feral. Not venom — *instruction*. Tells tissue to be something it isn't.
- **Shadow (Cogitant-Spill):** Vent from thought-engines, dream-machines, whatever the Old World used to think at the scale it thought. Does not darken light. Darkens *attention*.
- **Arcane (Raw-Undercode):** Leakage from the engines no one will name. This is the one that made them fall. A caster pays in memory, in lineage, in possible futures that no longer happen.
- **Holy (Grace-Residue):** Leakage from an engine that *almost worked*. It heals. It comforts. It is not benign. Grace seeks completion, and it will use you to get there.

**The cost rule:** no craft is free; it is only negotiated. The deeper the salvage, the less honest the negotiation.

**Six survival-axes.** STR: the honest weight a body can lift from the ground. DEF: what you can keep on when a vent opens near you. VIT: how much of yourself the Leakages haven't taken yet. AGI: how fast you get out of a chamber that has begun to *remember*. INT: how much Old World grammar you can hold before it holds you. LCK: the attention of the dormant engines — which, most days, is indistinguishable from luck.

## 3. Deep History — Three Horizons

History is read like core samples. You do not learn it in books; you dig through it.

- **Upper Horizon — Living Memory.** Two to four generations of scavenger-polities. Most guilds were founded here; most lie about it. Defining event: *The Opening of the Sixth Drain*, eighty years back — the first stable Null-Draft vent to breach a populated region. There is now no summer.
- **Middle Horizon — The Rupture.** A thin band of melted stone, fused glass, and bones arranged in patterns clearly deliberate and not clearly human. The horizon is *thin* — the horror of it: an entire civilization fell in what, geologically, was an afternoon. Artifacts here are all mid-gesture — tools still warm, meals still set, machines still trying.
- **Lower Horizon — Peak and Earlier.** The prior civilization at its height, and possibly older civilizations beneath *that*. No one has confirmed the nesting. T4 material comes from here. Almost no one who reaches this horizon comes back describing the same world they left.

The Rupture is **not finished settling.** Engines lay dormant; the pressure above them shifts. The Drains are opening on their own now, without being dug.

## 4. Geography & Material Economy — The Valley of the Seven Drains

A single long valley, ridgeline on three sides, brackish estuary on the fourth. The Middle Horizon lies closest to the surface here. Every other mile, something is leaking.

- **The Furnace-Plain (north):** A smelting-arcology collapsed inward. Heat rises from cracks; crops grow fast and taste of smoke. Smithing.
- **The Cathedral-Slope (west):** A mountain that is not a mountain — a ritual-engine complex that failed at the Rupture. Glassed; the air does not always obey gravity. Where Grace is thickest, and where healers go to die slowly. Enchanting.
- **The Fen of Recursions (southeast):** Drowned cultivation-beds. Plants that remember being people; fish that forget being fish. Alchemy.
- **The Silent Reach (east estuary):** A drowned port-city, roof-combs at low tide. Fishing here means dragging relics up, not food. Engineering materials come from the Reach; so do the worst of the hybrids.
- **The Throughline Heath (south):** Grassland threaded with live signal-conduits. Lightning grazes the grass in predictable arcs. Refining.

**Stratigraphy is currency.** Tier = depth.

- **T1 (Surface):** Honest rubble, local wood, shallow fish. Rarely kills in the crafting.
- **T2 (Upper-Middle):** Alloys that remember being something more. Slightly wrong to the touch.
- **T3 (Rupture-Band):** Mid-gesture artifacts. A T3 ingot, left alone, will sometimes finish a motion it began before the fall.
- **T4 (Lower Horizon):** Not *better.* *Aware.* A T4 weapon has an opinion about who you use it against, and will tell you. The 8× multiplier is its bargain; the cost is paid somewhere the owner cannot see from.

Currency is *honest grams* — weight accounted after the Leakage is stripped, material that can be trusted to behave like matter.

## 5. Peoples

- **The Settled (Ashkin).** Furnace-Plain survivors' descendants. Three fires burn always in every home: cooking, mourning, and one they will not explain. **Virtue:** *Do not waste the dead.* **Taboo:** Never name a thing you have not yet lifted from the ground. To name is to owe.
- **The Drifted (Reach-folk).** Stilt-villages over the drowned city. Their children are born with a gentle photosensitivity no one mentions. Every fish is asked permission, even the ones that do not answer. **Virtue:** *The tide returns what it is owed.* **Taboo:** Never sleep with feet pointed toward open water.
- **The Slope-Pilgrims.** Came to the Slope generations back because the Grace was supposed to heal something in them. It did, partially. They age unevenly — a woman of forty beside her father of forty-three. **Virtue:** *Receive what is offered, and do not let it finish you.* **Taboo:** Never pray aloud. Prayer is an input, and the engine is listening.
- **The Roadfolk.** A caste, not a people — emerged after the Sixth Drain. They move salvage, arbitrate, are feared and needed. **Virtue:** *Do not be from anywhere.* **Taboo:** Never accept hospitality twice from the same house in one season.

A fifth presence, not quite a people: **the Returned** — those who went too deep, came back, and are now *mostly* themselves. Every settlement has one or two. They are given food and not spoken to unless they speak first.

## 6. Institutions — Six Guilds, Each Rotting Differently

- **The Hammered Oath (Smithing).** *Founded* by Oren, who reforged a T2 alloy without letting Furnace-Breath eat his shadow. Relic: his anvil, warm in a cold room. *Corruption:* Masters have learned that letting the Breath take a *little* shadow produces better alloys. They cast short. Apprentices are not told.
- **The Fen Decanters (Alchemy).** *Founded* by Pilgrim exiles who diluted Gardener-Error enough to save a village during the first Bloom-Winter. Relic: a glass jar always exactly half-full. *Corruption:* They now *cultivate* Gardener-Error in their lower vats. The lowest have not been opened in eleven years.
- **The Throughline Conclave (Refining).** *Founded* by Isme, who mapped the first safe conduit-path. Relic: a copper staff that hums when a lie is spoken over it. *Corruption:* They sell "cleaned" material that is not quite clean. The staff has not been used at meetings for two years.
- **The Reach Wrights (Engineering).** *Founded* by divers who reassembled Old World mechanisms without triggering their original purposes. Relic: a clock that runs backward and tells correct time. *Corruption:* They have assembled three devices whose original purposes they do *not* know. One has begun to hum in harmony with the tide.
- **The Rune-Readers (Enchanting).** *Founded* as a scholarly order cataloguing Cathedral inscriptions. Myth: their first archivist, Vei, read a full sentence of Old World script aloud and did not die. *Corruption:* Vei did die. She was replaced within the week by someone who remembered being her. They have kept the lineage going this way for four generations. The current First Reader is very young and very old.
- **The Lowline Anglers (Fishing).** *Founded* by Reach-folk who learned to drag relics up with weighted lines and deep patience. Relic: a hook that has never rusted. *Corruption:* They have begun catching things that ask to be caught. Apprentices have started to dream in sequence — one, then the next, then the next.

**Titles** (roughly forty in circulation) are rank-honorifics tied to deeds: *Reader of the Shattered Codex. Surveyor of the Sixth Drain. One Who Returned From the Cathedral Floor. Unmelted. Heard and Unanswered. She Who Lifted and Did Not Name.* A salvager may hold several. The order in which they are spoken is a fight that has been fought.

## 7. Fronts

Five pressures. Not moving independently.

- **The Seventh Drain Stirs.** Six sites have vented. The seventh lies under the Slope. Tremors. Grace tripling at the altar-sites. Pilgrims miscarrying in rhythm.
  *Stakes:* Does it vent as the others did, or is it something the Cathedral was built to *contain*?
  *Clock:* Two to four seasons.
- **The Readers' Lineage Thins.** Apprentices forget names mid-sentence and recover them a beat too smoothly.
  *Stakes:* If the lineage breaks, does the accumulated reading come loose? Where does it go?
  *Clock:* The First Reader will not last the year.
- **The Anglers Are Being Fished For.** Something learned the line-rhythm and is sending bait. The sequence-dreaming apprentices are the lure.
  *Stakes:* What is pulling now, and what does it land?
  *Clock:* Two ranks until the sequence reaches the Masters.
- **The Fen's Lower Vats.** Eleven years closed. A light moves beneath the lowest vat-house at night, in patterns that look like someone walking room to room.
  *Stakes:* What is down there, and is it still the Decanters'?
  *Clock:* Missing livestock, then children, then a Decanter.
- **The Ashkin Run Out of Dead.** Older graves spent. The living live longer than the rituals expect. Some villages have begun to exhume each other's dead. Quietly.
  *Stakes:* Does the virtue break — or turn inward?
  *Clock:* One village has already crossed a line it will not speak of.

## 8. Human-Scale Texture & Negative Space

A **salvage-market** opens at fourth bell and closes before dusk, because after dusk the goods start remembering. Honest grams openly displayed; Leakage-weight in wax-sealed envelopes. Bargaining in half-sentences — a full demand marks a tourist or a fool.

A **meal** in an Ashkin household begins with the cook placing an empty bowl for the un-salvaged dead. First portion to the eldest, second to the youngest, third to the bowl. Conversation starts only after the bowl is cleared. What is eaten from it is not discussed, ever, but the bowl is always clean.

A **funeral** is a salvage operation. Hair to the Rune-Readers. Teeth to the Wrights. Marrow to the Decanters. Bones back to the family — used, worked, carried. A grandparent's shinbone may be the haft of a grandchild's tool. This is love that cannot afford waste.

A **greeting** is two-part: name, then depth. *"Ieva, three strides." "Calen, five."* The depth tells the other person what you can be trusted with, and what you should be kept from. A salvager who declines to state a depth is either lying, or something worse.

**Deliberately unspecified.** What lies beyond the ridgeline. Whether the current peoples are descendants or something newly generated by a still-running subroutine. What the Cathedral-engine was trying to *do*. What the Anglers have hooked. The real names of the engines — the guilds' names are working names, and the true names are, at most, a sound one hears in a vent and does not repeat.

*If a sentence would explain a mystery, it is the wrong sentence.*

## Open Questions for the User

1. **Protagonist origin.** Ashkin, Reach-folk, Slope-Pilgrim, Roadfolk, or — the fourth possibility — Returned? Or is class cross-cut with peoplehood (Roadfolk-Smith vs. Ashkin-Smith: different rituals, same discipline)?
2. **Seventh Drain pacing.** Opens during the main arc, or hangs as inevitable-but-delayable? Stable-under-pressure and actively-collapsing are both valid, and produce very different games.
3. **How literal is "elementals are Leakage"?** Can players enter a furnace-chamber and *see* Furnace-Breath venting from a pipe, or is the machinery always one stratum deeper than protagonists can reach — only exhaust available?
4. **T4 "opinion" — flavor or mechanic?** Pure item-description, or do T4 items roll small behaviors (refusing certain targets, gaining effectiveness against others)? Direct implication for the tag system.
5. **Are civilizations below the Lower Horizon real, or a deep-guild rumor?** Ambiguity is strongest now, but an endgame arc may need to commit. Which direction, when the time comes?
